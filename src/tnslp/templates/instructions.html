Map Exploration

The goal of the game is to leave The No-Shoelace Place. To leave you must explore the ward and other areas to find items and objects that you can use. Some of these items will be readily available for you to use, while others may be locked away or hidden. As you explore, you will be rewarded experience points (xp) for finding items and completing objectives, which will be important as you progress through the game.

Interaction with the Environment

The environment is made up of interconnected rooms built on a grid, meaning each room has rooms branching off from it in up to four directions. Movement between each room is dependent on the orientation of how the patient enters the room, and therefor the patient can go forward, backward, left and right. All items, including each room itself, can be inspected for more information about each space. Other items, when applicable, can be opened/closed, unlocked/locked, picked up/dropped, and other various actions.

Player Stats Panel

The patient stats panel gives real time information about how the patient is progressing through the game. The XP value denotes the current amount of experience that a patient has available to them. The day value shows the number of days the patient has been in The No-Shoelace Place and the turns left value shows the number of turns left in the current day. The room value shows the room the patient is in. The health value shows the current health of the patient. Finally, the status value shows the diagnosis that the patient received on arrival.

Player Abilities

There are 5 abilities available to the patient over the course of one's stay in The No-Shoelace Place: meditation, assertiveness, positive attitude, opposite action, and catharsis. Each day, coping skills groups are offered for patient participation where if, in attending, a patient has accumulated enough experience, they can learn, and level up these skills to for use throughout their stay at The No-Shoelace Place.
